History of Computer

 

By: Matthew Timothy Cabarle

 

 

Go to bottom

 Matthew Timothy Cabarle - Feb. 26, 2021

 Camarines Norte of State College

 

 

 

Ever since they originated, computers had greatly advanced from what they are like back then. They used to be bigger and slower, compared to the modern computers that we are using today. Let us now have a brief look back on the history of computers.

 

 

Apple II

 

 

 

Earliest Computer

 

 

The word computer was first recorded in 1613, which refers to a person who carries out calculations or computations.

 

Computers were originally a job title for those who make computations. These human computers were generally occupied on calculating mathematical expressions.

 

 

 

History of Computers

 

 

 

 

Tally Sticks

 

 

 

Tally sticks from the Swiss Alps: Alpbeilen from Alp Hinterschneit (Saanen BE), 1778.
Swiss Alpine Museum , CC BY 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

A tally stick was an ancient mnemonic device that aids on recording and documenting numbers, quantities, and even messages.

 

 

 

 

Abacus

 

 

 

 

 

An abacus is mechanical device used to help individuals in performing mathematical computations like arithmetic operations. It was invented in Babylonia in 2400 B.C.

 

 

 

 

Napiers Bones

 

 

 

An 18th century set of Napier's Bones.
Stephencdickson, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

It was invented by John Napier in 1614. It allows the user to multiply, divide, and calculate square and cube roots by moving the rods around and placing them in specially constructed board.

 

 

 

 

Slide Rule

 

 

 

 

 

It was invented by William Oughtred in 1622, and was based on Napiers ideas about logarithms. It was used primarily for multiplication, division, roots, logarithms, and trigonometry, but not regularly used for addition and subtraction.

 

 

 

 

Pascaline

 

 

Six figures calculating machine by Blaise Pascal without sous or deniers.
Rama, CC BY-SA 3.0 FR, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The Pascaline or also called Arithmetic Machine, the first calculator or adding machine to be produced in any quantity and actually used. This was designed and built by the French mathematician-philosopher Blaise Pascal between 1642 and 1644.

 

 

 

 

Step Reckoner

 

 

 

Replica of a calculating machine from 1700 by de: Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz.

User:Kolossos, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The step reckoner (or stepped reckoner) was the first calculator that could perform all four arithmetic operations. It was invented by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in 1672.

 

 

 

 

Jacquard Loom

 

 

 

Jacquard loom surmounted by a Verdol mechanism.
Musee des Arts et Metiers , CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The Jacquard Loom is a mechanical loom that used interchangeable punch cards to perform automated tasks. It was invented by Joseph Marie Jacquard in 1881.

 

 

 

 

Arithmometer

 

 

 

One of the first Arithmometers with unique serial number (around 1865).

Ezrdr, CC BY 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The Arithmometer is a mechanical calculator invented by Thomas de Colmar in 1820. It is the first reliable, useful and commercially successful calculating machine, that could perform the four basic mathematical functions.

 

 

 

 

Difference Engine and Analytical Engine

 

 

 

Babbage Difference Engine No. 2
Canticle at English Wikipedia, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Analytical Engine.
Science Museum London / Science and Society Picture Library, CC BY-SA 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons.

 

 

 

The first mechanical calculator. It is an automatic, mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions. This was invented by Charles Babbage in 1822 and 1834.

 

 

 

 

First Computer Programmer

 

 

 

 

In 1840, Augusta Ada Byron suggests to Babbage that he use the binary system.

 

 

She writes programs for the Analytical Engine.

 

Watercolor portrait of Ada Lovelace.
By Alfred Edward Chalon , Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

 

Scheutzian Calculation Engine

 

 

 

Scheutz's calculator.
Charles Brooke, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The first printing calculator, invented by Per Georg Scheutz in 1843. This was based on Charles Babbages difference engine.

 

 

 

 

Tabulating Machine

 

 

 

Hollerith 1890 tabulating machine with sorting box.
Adam Schuster, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The tabulating machine was invented by Herman Hollerith in 1890. It is designed to be used in summarizing information stored in punched cards.

 

 

 

 

Harvard Mark I

 

 

 

Harvard-IBM Mark I Computer, detail of Input/Output and control.
The original uploader was Daderot at English Wikipedia., CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The Harvard Mark One or also known as IBM Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator (ASCC). It was the first electro-mechanical computer invented by Howard H. Aiken in 1943.

 

 

 

 

The First Computer Bug

 

 

 

An actual bug in a computer log, having been found in the relays of a computer in 1947.
Courtesy of the Naval Surface Warfare Center, Dahlgren, VA., 1988., Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

Grace Hopper, one of the primary programmers for the Mark I, was credited for finding the first computer bug. It was a body of a dead moth that found its way into the Mark II. Since circa 1889, the word bug has been used to describe a defect, but Hopper was credited with coining the word debugging to describe the work to eliminate program faults.

 

 

 

 

Z1

 

 

 

1989 reconstruction of the Zuse Z1 at the German Museum of Technology in Berlin.
Morn, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

Z1 is the first programmable computer and was invented by Konrad Zuse in Germany from 1936 to 1938. To program the Z1, the user needs to insert a punch tape into the punch tape reader. The output was also generated through punch tape.

 

 

 

 

Atanasoff-Berry Computer (ABC)

 

 

 

Atanasoff-Berry Computer.
Ik T from Kanagawa, Japan, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The first electronic digital computing device. It was invented by Professor John Atanasoff and graduate student Clifford Berry at Iowa State University between 1939 and 1942.

 

 

 

 

ENIAC

 

 

 

Cpl. Irwin Goldstein (foreground) sets the switches on one of the ENIAC's function tables at the Moore School of Electrical Engineering. (U.S. Army photo).
Unidentified U.S. Army photographer, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

ENIAC was an acronym for Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer. It was the first electric general-purpose computer, developed by John Presper Eckert and John W. Mauchly.

 

 

 

 

UNIVAC I

 

 

 

U.S. Census Bureau employees tabulate data using one of the agencys UNIVAC computers, ca. 1960.
U.S. Census Bureau employees, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The UNIVAC I (UNIVersal Automatic Computer 1) was the first commercial computer. Designed by J. Presper Eckert and John Mauchly.

 

 

 

 

EDVAC

 

 

 


U.S. Army Photo of EDVAC as installed in BRL building 328.
See page for author, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

EDVAC stands for Electronic Discrete Variable Automatic Computer. It was the first stored-program computer that has a memory to hold both a stored program as well as data. It was designed by Von Neumann in 1952.

 

 

 

 

The First Portable Computer

 

 

 

The Osborne 1 is the first commercially successful portable microcomputer. It was released on April 3, 1981 by Osborne Computer Corporation.

Osborne 1 portable computer - early model.
Bilby, CC BY 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons
